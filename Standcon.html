Faq

FAQs about STANDCON and STANDCON CTF.

Who can participate in STANDCON?

All tertiary (i.e. Polytechnic, ITE and University)  students, pre-enlistees and NSF are welcome to join STANDCON. Pre-enlistees and NSF can sign up with your highest attained qualification.

How will COVID-19 restrictions affect STANDCON?

All STANDCON activities will be held virtually this year to protect our participants.

Can I join STANDCON without attending the CTF?

Yes. Particpants are welcome to sign up for only the webinar portion of the event in our sign up form. 

Can participants from different schools form a team for STANDCON CTF? 

Yes, teams from different schools are allowed to sign up together as a team. 

Can I participate in STANDCON CTF if I have less than 4 members in my team?

Yes, you can register as a team of less than 4 members. The organizers will assist in matching you with other registrants where possible.

The Cybersecurity Eco-System in Singapore and How You Can Get Involved in It  

BY STEVEN WONG

Dr. Steven Wong will highlight the various aspects of the cybersecurity ecosystem, including education, community, industry, and regional opportunities. He will also mention how students can get involved and contribute to the Singapore cybersecurity eco-system, including some of AISP’s past/current activities.

The Evolving Landscape of Cloud Security

BY EDWINDER SINGH

As more enterprise workloads move to the cloud, there's a need to understand security to the Cloud, security on the Cloud and security in the Cloud. In his webinar, Edwinder will provide further insights on their differences.

Importance of Manual Penetration Testing

BY TJ NULL

As corporations use automated tools to conduct tasks and improve performance of their work, many findings from these automated tools can lead to false positives or provide misinformation. 

In this talk, TJ will explain the importance of conducting manual testing and explain why it is important to understand the tools you use in your operations.
 
In addition, TJ will also provide tips and resources to verify the tools you use and provide guidance on how you can create your own tools.

Car Hacking 101: How to get started

BY ALINA TAN

Looking to start your journey in Car Hacking? Don't know where to start? 

This talk serves as a starting guideline that teaches you the basics of vehicular architecture, vehicular security protocols, and the different open-source simulation tools to understand more about the practical aspect of the vehicular CAN bus.

The talk will also address how to build a physical test bench with limited components to understand more about vehicular architectures.

Cybersecurity Knowledge in a Non-Cybersecurity World  

BY PEI SI TAN

Meet Pei Si, a Hardware Operations Engineer in Google. Pei Si will be sharing about her experiences in the industry. This session will highlight the significance of having a cybersecurity knowledge in her career, and how it has helped her excel in her job. 

Threat Intelligence & Visibility

BY ANDREW ONG

Threat intelligence is used to understand a threat actor's motives, targets and attack behaviours. Andrew will share his insights on how digital data is leveraged as "Threat Intelligence" and its impact on a daily basis.

Elements of Strong Security Architectures

BY RAYDEN CHIA

For many enterprise architecture decision makers, a major challenge is trying to balance being stack-agnostic, being modern, and being secure. However, it is not a trade-off in that one may only pick two of the three.

In this talk, Rayden will work with you in trying to develop a mental framework to help design modern architectures and look at some of the key elements that make them secure, while preserving other desirable system properties.

Red Team: The Art of Information Gathering 
(OSINT and Social Engineering)

BY GLENICE TAN

Red teaming helps to measure the organization's readiness by simulating real life attacks. To outsmart our enemies, one way is to think like them! Most red team exercises starts with little to no information. Thus, the initial reconnaissance and foothold is crucial for the planning and execution of subsequent operations. This leads to the questions:

1. How do the red team engineers gather information?
2. Using these information, how do they gain an initial foothold?

In this session, Glenice will be sharing about the initial phases of red teaming, in particular, Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) and Social Engineering. In addition, with the increase in online fraud (e.g. phishing) and identity theft, it is important to examine our Internet footprints.

By the end of the session, you will learn the basics of OSINT, social engineering practices and some tips to better protect ourselves.
