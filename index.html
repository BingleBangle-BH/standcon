The Cybersecurity Eco-System in Singapore 

Dr. Steven Wong will highlight the various aspects of the cybersecurity ecosystem, including education, community, industry, and regional opportunities. He will also mention how students can get involved and contribute to the Singapore cybersecurity eco-system, including some of AISPâ€™s past/current activities.

Steven Wong, Associate Professor, Singapore Institute of Technology

Importance of Manual Penetration Testing

As corporations use automated tools to conduct tasks and improve performance of their work, many findings from these automated tools can lead to false positives or provide misinformation. In this talk, TJ will explain the importance of conducting manual testing and explain why it is important to understand the tools you use in your operations. In addition, TJ will also provide tips and resources to verify the tools you use and provide guidance on how you can create your own tools.

TJ Null, Offensive Security

Evolving Landscape of Cloud Security

As more enterprise workloads move to the cloud, there's a need to understand security to the Cloud, security on the Cloud and security in the Cloud. In his webinar, Edwinder will provide further insights on their differences.

Edwinder Singh, Global Hybrid Cloud Advisory Director, NTT Ltd.

Car Hacking 101: How to get started

The thought of having a hacker remotely seizing control of your vehicle sounds like an action film. However, car hacking is definitely real and is seeping into our daily lives. alina, founder of car security quarter (CSQ) will introduce the widely discussed topic.

Alina Tan, Founder, Car Security Quarter(CSQ)

Threat Intelligence & Visibility

Threat intelligence have been used to understand a threat actor's motives, targets and attack behaviours. Andrew will share his insights on How digital data is leveraged as "Threat Intelligence" and its impact on a daily basis.

Andrew Wong, Regional Sales Manager, Cyberint

Elements of Strong Security Architectures

For many enterprise architecture decision makers, a major challenge is trying to balance being stack-agnostic, being modern, and being secure. However, it is not a tradeoff in that one may only pick two of the three. In this talk, Rayden will try to develop a mental framework to help design modern architectures and look at some of the key elements that make them secure, while preserving other desirable system properties.

Rayden Chia, Security Engineer, Google

Cybersecurity Knowledge in a Non-Cybersecurity World

Meet Pei Si, a Hardware Operations Engineer in Google. Pei Si will be sharing about her experiences in the industry. This session will highlight the significance of having a cybersecurity knowledge in her career, and how it has helped her excel in her job. 

Pei Si, Hardware Operations Engineer, Google

Red Team: The Art of Information Gathering

Red teaming helps to measure the organization's readiness by simulating real life attacks. To outsmart our enemies, one way is to think like them! In this session, Glenice will be sharing about the initial phases of red teaming, in particular, Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) and Social Engineering. In addition, with the increase in online fraud (e.g. phishing) and identity theft, it is important to examine our Internet footprints. By the end of the session, you will learn the basics of OSINT, social engineering practices and some tips to better protect ourselves.

Glenice Tan,  Associate Cybersecurity Specialist, Cyber Security Group Government Technology Agency
